## What do you actually do day to day?

I turn business questions into data answers, and increasingly I build the AI that does
that work alongside me: retrieval chatbots over our data, agents that write and run SQL,
and pipelines that generate insights and charts automatically.

## What are you best at?

Honestly, it's gathering requirements until they're clear on both ends, then turning them
into solutions by leveraging AI. It's human nature to kick off with an ambiguous question
rather than a documented spec. It's like walking into a car dealership: you don't show up
with a full specification, you show up with a rough sense of what you want and a budget.
Data work is the same. Someone wants to track a metric they can't quite define yet, so I
break it down, draw the metric out on a whiteboard, and ask questions that mostly land on a
simple yes or no. That makes it easier for them, and it gets us to something real, fast.

## What's it like to work with you?

I like to nail down the requirement before building anything, so expect a few clarifying
questions up front. It can look like friction, but it saves a lot of rework later and keeps
everything documented. If the back-and-forth stalls, I'll pull people into a quick
whiteboarding session to align. And I tend to build proactively: I watch how people work,
spot the recurring problems, and put something in front of them before they even ask.

## Why the focus on AI and analytics?

Used properly, AI is a huge productivity multiplier for analytics work, and I think any
analyst who doesn't embrace it will get left behind by the ones who genuinely know how to
use it (not the ones just churning out slop). The part that excites me is making an agent
genuinely useful on real, messy company data instead of a clean demo.

## What tools and stack do you use?

Presto SQL and Python are the core, plus LangChain, RAG, and multi-agent systems for the AI
work, and Claude agents and skills. On the analytics side it's A/B testing, dashboarding,
and data visualization. Day to day I build with tools like Claude Code and Cursor. But in my time with Shopee, most of these are done within inhouse tech stack.

## What are you most interested in right now?

The overlap between analytics and AI agents: how to make an agent genuinely useful on
real, messy company data instead of a demo.

## What are you learning right now, and how do you keep up with AI?

My approach is pretty hands-on: I think of a modern problem and just build it with AI. I
learn while building, and when I hit a concept I don't understand, I'll pull up a YouTube
video to help illustrate it. Building and learning at the same time keeps it practical.
